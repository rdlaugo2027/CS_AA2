Bittersweet Verses: A Love Unraveled

A Shakespearean Sonnet By: Ruzzel Angelo Laugo


In solitude, I'd rather dwell, apart,

Sans your presence, my heart finds reprieve.

You held my tempest thoughts, a saving art,

Yet tore my heart, unasked, you chose to cleave.


You, my cherished anguish, causing pain,

through the ache, emotions start to flow.

Upon my skin, your whispers leave a stain,

Your loving scars within, a tender show.


Despite the rending of my longing heart,

Desire fulfilled, a twisted bliss.

Yet you, with hold upon my soul, impart,

I wish to part from your life's dark abyss.


No more the same, I've changed in every part,

Break not just my heart, but our bond, dismiss.
