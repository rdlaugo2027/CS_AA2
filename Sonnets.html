Sonnet Writing: Conflicts in Love

A Ballad of Conflicts in Love (A Sonnet by Raphael Abes)

Inspired by the song "Rewrite The Stars" by Zendaya and Zac Efron from the movie "The Greatest Showman", this Sonnet encompasses the theme of conflicts in love. After reading a short piece of art, you'll be inside the view and perspective of 2 lovers who's massive differences forbid them to be together. While overall a sad concept, the message it conveys is highly meaningful and emotional.

Torn Between Two Hearts (A Sonnet by Raphael Abes)

Based on the song "Someone Like You" by Adele, Torn Between Two Hearts brings you to the world of a person who still hasn't gotten over a previous heartbreak. Constantly bringing back thoughts of the past which continues to hurt them even more. They soon get an imaginary figure of their ex-lover, which results in a moment of happiness, yet quickly turns into eternal sadness. A deep message that's sure to strike the emotions of readers.

Echoes of Vanishing Love (A Sonnet by Ruzzel laugo)

The sonnet titled "Echoes of Vanishing Love" is based on the song "Romantic Homicide" by d4vd. It explores the emotional turmoil and questioning that arise when one feels neglected or abandoned in a relationship. The speaker expresses a deep sense of fear and seeks enlightenment from their beloved, pondering the reasons behind the fading connection. There is a haunting reflection on the absence of emotional response, even to the imagined demise of the relationship.

Bittersweet Verses: A Love Unraveled (A Sonnet by Ruzzel Laugo)

The sonnet, titled "Bittersweet Verses: A Love Unraveled," Is based on the song "I Would Rather Live Alone (I'm Not Who I'm Today)" by IV of Spades. It explores the intricate and contradictory nature of love. It delves into the poet's experience of finding solace and pain in a relationship, where moments of bliss are interwoven with heartache. The verses reflect on the paradoxical emotions that arise from the tumultuous journey of love, capturing the essence of both joy and sorrow.
